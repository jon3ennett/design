Industrial Design

My Role | Design lead for extreme duty clamps for the world's most powerful production jump starter.

Target Market | Heavy duty industrial 12V and 24V equipment, 18-wheelers, buses, and more.

Design Prompt | Combine strengths of exsiting clamps for usability and power transfer. We were given 3-4 months from concept to mass manufacture.

I jumped into CAD immediately as we had a solid backbone with the other two clamps already modeled.

Version 1.0

Ergonomic handle with "slim-nosed" clamp. Shown here is a portion of the back and forth with manufacturers specifying manufacturing techniques.

3D print for study of ergonomics, parts fit, and details.

Version 2.0

After updating details, we moved forward to a soft-tooling short production run for field study.

Initial Field Tests | Engineering Feedback

1 | Cable was pulling loose from crimp.
2 | Clamp welded shut with contact to grounded surface during live test.
3 & 4 | Needed a smaller radius in teeth arc for better grip on posts and bolts.

Concept Failed.

Version 3.0

Updates

1 | Updated CAD to include rivet cover.
2 | Updated crimping procedure with manufacturer.
3 | Decreased radius in teeth arc, increased tooth size, and improved tooth shape.

Intermediate Field Tests | Engineering Feedback

1 | Teeth fit around post better, but pushed post upward when attemping to "grab."
2 & 3 | Screenshots from engineers' testing video showing clamp holding double its weight. Better than previous design.
4 | Meeting with engineers to discuss further clamp teeth development.

Concept Failed.

Version 4.0  (Final)

Updates

1 | Rivet cover thickness updated for user protection, design updated for appearance and manufacturability.
2 | Top teeth added. Tooth row design updated to have upper teeth facing downwards, lower teeth facing upwards -
      improved functionality.
3 | Radius of arc in teeth was reduced for more circular profile for improved grip and surface contact.

Final Field Tests | Engineering Feedback

1 | Manufacturable brass teeth for final testing rounds.
2 | Teeth held snug to battery bolts and allowed for maximum power current from jump starter.
3 & 4 | Engineers could not pull clamp off of standard sized jump post.

Concept Passed | Production deadline hit.
